### Manual Entry — Step by Step

1. **Open the Dashboard** in your browser
2. **Select a date** (defaults to today)
3. **Enter your bedtime** — when you went to bed (24h format, e.g., `22:30`)
4. **Enter your wake time** — when you woke up (e.g., `06:00`)
5. **Rate your sleep quality** — 1 to 5 stars (optional but helpful)
6. **Add notes** — anything about your sleep (optional)
7. **Submit** — your hours are calculated automatically

### AI Screenshot Upload — The Easy Way

If you use a wearable (Apple Watch, Oura Ring, Fitbit, Whoop, Garmin), you
can skip manual entry:

1. **Take a screenshot** of your sleep data from your wearable app
2. **Click "Upload Screenshot"** on the Dashboard
3. **Our AI reads it** — it extracts everything automatically:
   - Total sleep time
   - Bedtime and wake time
   - Sleep score
   - Deep sleep, REM, light sleep durations
   - Sleep efficiency
   - Time to fall asleep
4. **Review and submit** — the form fills itself in

## How Your Sleep Score Works

Your **Composite Sleep Score** is a 0-100 rating that captures the *full
picture* of your sleep — not just how long, but how well.

```
  ╔════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
  ║                                                            ║
  ║              YOUR COMPOSITE SLEEP SCORE                    ║
  ║              ──────────────────────────                    ║
  ║                                                            ║
  ║     ┌─────────────────────────────────────┐     0-100      ║
  ║     │█████████████████████████████░░░░░░░░│     SCORE      ║
  ║     └─────────────────────────────────────┘                ║
  ║                                                            ║
  ║     Built from up to 5 research-backed dimensions:         ║
  ║                                                            ║
  ║     ████████████████████  DURATION        35%              ║
  ║     ██████████████        CONSISTENCY     25%              ║
  ║     ████████              EFFICIENCY      15%  ┐           ║
  ║     ████████              SLEEP STAGES    15%  │ wearable  ║
  ║     █████                 LATENCY         10%  ┘           ║
  ║                                                            ║
  ╚════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

### The Five Dimensions

#### 1. Duration (35% of your score)

How long you slept. The sweet spot is **7.0 to 8.5 hours**.

```
  HOURS      0    5    6   6.5   7   7.5   8   8.5   9   9.5+
  SCORE      0    0    0    50  100  100  100  100   70    70
             ─────────── ────── ──────────────────── ─────────
              Too little  Ramp    ★ Full Credit ★    Oversleep
                          up                         penalty
```

- Below 6 hours: 0 points (your body needs more)
- 6 to 7 hours: partial credit (ramping up)
- **7.0 to 8.5 hours: full credit** (the science-backed optimal range)
- Above 8.5 hours: gentle penalty (oversleeping can indicate issues)

> **Research:** AASM (2015) and NSF (2015) recommend 7-9 hours for adults.
> The 7.5-hour target aligns with exactly 5 complete 90-minute sleep cycles.

#### 2. Consistency (25% of your score)

How regular your bed and wake times are across the last 7 days. Your body's
circadian rhythm *craves* routine.

```
  VARIATION       ≤30 min    30-60 min   60-90 min   90+ min
  SCORE            100         70           40          0
                  ──────── ─────────── ─────────── ──────────
                  Excellent    Good        Fair        Poor

  Same bedtime every night = higher score
  Erratic schedule = lower score
```

> **Research:** Phillips et al. (2017) found that irregular sleep schedules
> were associated with poorer academic performance and delayed circadian
> rhythms — even when total sleep time was adequate.

#### 3. Efficiency (15% of your score) — *wearable data*

The percentage of time in bed that you actually spent **sleeping**.

```
  EFFICIENCY      90%+       85-90%      80-85%      <80%
  SCORE            100         80          50          20
```

If you're in bed for 8 hours but only sleeping 6.5, your efficiency is ~81%.
The goal is to make your bed a place for *sleep*, not scrolling.

> **Research:** Ohayon et al. (2004) established that healthy sleepers
> typically achieve 85%+ sleep efficiency. Below 80% may indicate insomnia.

#### 4. Sleep Stages (15% of your score) — *wearable data*

The quality breakdown: are you getting enough **deep sleep** and **REM**?

```
  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │                                                        │
  │  A healthy night of sleep:                             │
  │                                                        │
  │  ░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░  Light Sleep (50-60%)   │
  │  ████████████████               Deep Sleep  (15-25%)   │
  │  ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓           REM Sleep   (20-25%)   │
  │                                                        │
  │  Deep Sleep = physical recovery, immune function       │
  │  REM Sleep  = memory, learning, emotional regulation   │
  │                                                        │
  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

| Stage | Target | Full Credit | Partial Credit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deep Sleep | 15%+ of total | 100 points | 60 points (10-15%) |
| REM Sleep | 20%+ of total | 100 points | 60 points (15-20%) |

> **Research:** Ohayon et al. (2004) characterized normal sleep architecture
> across age groups. Deep sleep (N3/slow-wave) is critical for physical
> restoration; REM is essential for cognitive processing and memory.

#### 5. Latency (10% of your score) — *wearable data*

How quickly you fall asleep.

```
  MINUTES       <5 min     5-20 min    20-30 min    30+ min
  SCORE           60         100          60           20
                ──────── ─────────── ─────────── ──────────
                Sleep debt   ★ Ideal ★    Slow      Insomnia
                 warning                             red flag
```

Falling asleep *too fast* (under 5 minutes) actually signals sleep debt —
your body is so deprived it shuts down immediately. The ideal is 5-20 minutes.

> **Research:** Phillips et al. (2017) established that sleep onset latency
> is a reliable indicator of underlying sleep health.

### No Wearable? No Problem.

If you don't have a wearable device, your score is calculated from the two
dimensions available to everyone:

```
  WITHOUT WEARABLE:

     ██████████████████████████████   DURATION       60%
     ████████████████████             CONSISTENCY    40%
                                                    ────
                                                    100%
```

The scoring system **dynamically adjusts** — it only weights dimensions
where you have real data. You're never penalized for missing wearable metrics.

## Why 7.5 Hours? The Research

```
  ╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
  ║                                                           ║
  ║     WHY 7.5 HOURS?                                        ║
  ║                                                           ║
  ║     One sleep cycle = ~90 minutes                         ║
  ║                                                           ║
  ║     ┌──────┐ ┌──────┐ ┌──────┐ ┌──────┐ ┌──────┐        ║
  ║     │Cycle │ │Cycle │ │Cycle │ │Cycle │ │Cycle │        ║
  ║     │  1   │ │  2   │ │  3   │ │  4   │ │  5   │        ║
  ║     │90 min│ │90 min│ │90 min│ │90 min│ │90 min│        ║
  ║     └──────┘ └──────┘ └──────┘ └──────┘ └──────┘        ║
  ║                                                           ║
  ║     5 cycles x 90 minutes = 450 minutes = 7.5 HOURS      ║
  ║                                                           ║
  ║     Completing full cycles (not waking mid-cycle)         ║
  ║     is how you wake up feeling refreshed.                 ║
  ║                                                           ║
  ╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

Each sleep cycle takes you through four stages:

```
  One 90-Minute Sleep Cycle:

  AWAKE  ─────╮
              │
  LIGHT  ─────┼──╮                    ╭──────
  (N1/N2)     │  │                    │
              │  │                    │
  DEEP   ─────│──┼──╮           ╭────╯
  (N3)        │  │  │           │
              │  │  ╰───────────╯
  REM    ─────│──╯
              │
  ────────────┼──────────────────────────────
              0       30       60       90 min
```

- **Light Sleep (N1/N2):** Transition phase — heart rate drops, muscles relax
- **Deep Sleep (N3):** Physical restoration — tissue repair, immune boosting, growth hormone release
- **REM Sleep:** Mental restoration — memory consolidation, emotional processing, creativity

The first cycles are deep-sleep heavy. Later cycles are REM heavy. That's why
cutting sleep short steals your REM — and why **getting all 5 cycles matters**.

### The Research Behind Our Benchmarks

| Source | Year | What They Found | How We Use It |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AASM** (American Academy of Sleep Medicine) | 2015 | Adults need 7+ hours regularly for optimal health | Set our daily goal at 7.5h (5 full cycles) |
| **NSF** (National Sleep Foundation) | 2015 | Recommended 7-9 hours for ages 18-64 | Full scoring credit for 7.0-8.5 hours |
| **Phillips et al.** | 2017 | Irregular sleep schedules impair performance even with adequate total sleep | Consistency weighted at 25% of composite score |
| **Ohayon et al.** | 2004 | Established normative sleep architecture — healthy deep/REM/efficiency ranges across age groups | Used their benchmarks for efficiency (85%+) and stage percentage targets |

### The Cost of Not Sleeping

After just **one night** of poor sleep (< 6 hours):
- Reaction time drops by **300%** (worse than being legally drunk)
- Willpower decreases significantly
- Appetite-regulating hormones shift toward hunger
- Immune function drops by up to **70%**

After **one week** of 6-hour nights:
- Cognitive performance matches someone who hasn't slept for **48 hours straight**
- Hundreds of genes change their expression patterns
- Blood sugar regulation resembles pre-diabetes

### Sleep at Work

Research consistently shows that well-rested teams:
- Make **fewer errors** in judgment and communication
- Show **higher creativity** in problem-solving
- Report **better morale** and collaboration
- Experience **less burnout** and turnover
